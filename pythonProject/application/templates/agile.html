Agile is a set of principles for software development under which requirements and solutions evolve through the collaborative effort of self-organizing and cross-functional teams. It promotes adaptive planning, evolutionary development, early delivery, continuous improvement, and encourages flexible responses to change.

Roles in Agile

  • Product Owner: The Product Owner represents the customer and ensures that the team is working on the right things from a business perspective.
  • Scrum Master: The Scrum Master is responsible for ensuring the team follows Agile practices and removing any obstacles to the team's progress.
  • Development Team: The team is made up of professionals who deliver the product increment. They are self-organizing and cross-functional.

Scrum

Scrum is a framework that helps teams work together. It encourages teams to learn through experiences, self-organize while working on a problem, and reflect on their wins and losses to continuously improve.

  • Sprint: A time-boxed period during which specific work has to be completed and made ready for review.
  • Daily Scrum: A short daily meeting for the development team to discuss progress, challenges, and plan for the day.
  • Sprint Review: A meeting at the end of the sprint to inspect the increment and adapt the product backlog if needed.
  • Sprint Retrospective: A meeting at the end of each sprint where the team discusses what went well, what didn't, and how to improve.

Kanban

Kanban is a visual framework for implementing Agile that focuses on the continuous delivery of work, ensuring that the team is not overburdened, and increasing efficiency by reducing bottlenecks in the workflow.

  • Kanban Board: A visual tool that shows work in various stages of the process, often using columns like 'To Do', 'In Progress', and 'Done'.
  • Work In Progress (WIP) Limits: Limits set to the amount of work that can be in progress at any one time to help prevent bottlenecks.
  • Continuous Delivery: The practice of frequently delivering working software, usually in small increments.
  • Cycle Time: The time taken from the start of a task to its completion, which teams aim to reduce to improve efficiency.
