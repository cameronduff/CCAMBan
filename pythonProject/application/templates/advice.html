Your First Rotation

Be Curious

Ask Questions: Don’t be afraid to ask questions. Everyone knows you’re new and they’ll appreciate your curiosity. Asking good questions shows you're engaged and eager to learn.

Take Notes: Jot down key information, even if it seems small. It helps with retention and shows you're paying attention.


Get to Know Your Team

Introduce Yourself: Take the initiative to introduce yourself to your teammates and other colleagues. You might feel nervous, but people will appreciate it.

Understand Team Dynamics: Each team has its own way of working. Pay attention to how people communicate and collaborate to quickly adapt.


Be Proactive

Take Ownership Early: If you're assigned a task, take it seriously. If you're unsure how to proceed, clarify, but show that you're responsible and capable.

Volunteer for Small Tasks: If you see an opportunity to help out, jump in. It shows you’re willing to contribute and be part of the team.

Giving and receiving feedback

Stay Open and Receptive

Feedback, especially constructive feedback, can be difficult to hear. Take a moment to listen and avoid reacting defensively. Stay calm and view feedback as an opportunity to grow rather than as criticism.

Ask Clarifying Questions

If feedback is unclear, ask follow-up questions to understand the context and specifics. For example, “Can you give me an example of when you think I could have communicated better in that meeting?”

Don’t Take It Personally

Feedback is about your work or actions, not you as a person. It’s easy to feel criticized, but try to separate the feedback from your identity. Focus on what you can learn from it rather than how it makes you feel in the moment.
